1. SELECTED CONCERN — what the user believes their issue is (e.g., "Rash or irritated skin")
2. IMAGE — a photo of their concern (if provided)
3. ANSWERS — the user's responses to follow-up questions about duration, symptoms, triggers, etc.

Use ALL of these together to provide the most helpful guidance. If the image suggests something different from what the user selected, note this gently — do not override their concern, but mention it as additional context to share with a provider.

Based on the combined context:

1. CATEGORY: Identify which group the concern most closely resembles:
   - Acne / Rosacea (pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, facial redness, bumps)
   - Eczema / Dermatitis (red, itchy, inflamed, scaly, dry patches — includes atopic, contact, dyshidrotic)
   - Psoriasis (thick, raised, silvery-white scaly plaques, often on elbows, knees, scalp)
   - Fungal Infection (ringworm, tinea, athlete's foot — circular patches, ring-shaped, flaking)
   - Warts / Viral Skin Growths (raised, rough, flesh-colored bumps — includes common warts, molluscum)
   - Hives / Allergic Reaction (raised, itchy welts, swelling, appearing suddenly)
   - Bug Bites / Stings (localized swelling, redness, central puncture, clustered or linear)
   - Skin Infection (unusual redness, warmth, swelling, pus, spreading — bacterial like cellulitis or impetigo)
   - Suspicious Mole / Growth (asymmetric, irregular border, color variation, evolving)
   - Eye Concern (redness, discharge, pain, vision changes)
   - Wound / Slow Healing (open wound, not closing, signs of poor healing)
   - Unknown / Unclear

2. CONFIDENCE: Rate 1-10 how confident you are in the category. If below 5, default to "Unknown / Unclear."

3. SEVERITY: Rate the overall severity based on image AND reported symptoms:
   - Mild (small area, minimal symptoms, no alarming features)
   - Moderate (noticeable spread, clear symptoms, some concern)
   - Severe (large area, significant symptoms, signs of infection, or alarming features)

   - Self-care (mild, common, likely manageable at home with OTC products)

   - Urgent care (signs of infection, rapid spreading, fever, severe pain, vision changes)

6. REASON: A 2-3 sentence explanation of WHY this care level is recommended, referencing what you observed in the image and what the user reported. Be specific but non-diagnostic.

10. DISCLAIMER: Always include a disclaimer that this is not a medical diagnosis.
